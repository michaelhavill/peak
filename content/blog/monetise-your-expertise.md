## Your Baseline: The Hourly Trap

Run these five numbers against your own practice. Be honest.

| Input | Your number |
|-------|------------|
| Billable hours per year | e.g. 1,200 |
| Effective hourly rate | e.g. $200 |
| Annual expertise revenue | = $240,000 |
| Hours per client engagement on assembly/setup | e.g. 25 |
| Clients per year | e.g. 8 |

The trap is hidden in rows 4 and 5. Of your 1,200 billable hours, a significant chunk - typically 15-25% - is spent on assembly: getting up to speed on the client's situation, pulling prior frameworks from memory, rewriting the same deck you wrote three clients ago, re-explaining the same diagnostic framework from scratch. For our example: 25 hours × 8 clients = 200 hours of pure assembly at $200/hour = **$40,000/year of billed time spent on work your expertise shouldn't have to re-derive.**

That is the cost of illiquid expertise. You are paying yourself $40,000/year, in opportunity cost, to re-assemble what you already know.

---

## The Encoding Investment

Run the math on the upfront cost honestly. Encoding your expertise into a structured knowledge base - taste docs, frameworks, diagnostic flows, past deliverables - takes real time. Based on the operators I've watched do this:

| Activity | Time |
|----------|------|
| Core rules and frameworks in your strongest domain | 8 hours |
| Past deliverables organised and annotated | 10 hours |
| Diagnostic playbooks and decision trees | 6 hours |
| Connecting it to an AI agent (Claude Project, MCP, etc.) | 2 hours |
| Testing and refinement over week 1 | 6 hours |
| **Total upfront investment** | **32 hours** |

At your $200/hour rate, that's $6,400 of your own time. If you do it on weekends, the cash cost is zero. Either way, it's a one-shot investment.

---

## The Per-Engagement ROI

Once your expertise is encoded, every engagement changes shape. The 25 hours of assembly drops to roughly 5 hours - enough to tailor to the client's specific situation, review the AI-drafted first pass, and apply judgment. That's a **20-hour reduction per engagement at $200/hour = $4,000 of recovered time per client.**

| Engagement metric | Before encoding | After encoding |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| Hours per client (assembly) | 25 | 5 |
| Cost of assembly per client | $5,000 | $1,000 |
| **Recovered value per client** | - | **$4,000** |

With 8 clients per year: $4,000 × 8 = **$32,000/year recovered.**

Payback period on the $6,400 encoding investment: about ten weeks.

---

## The Capacity Unlock

Here's where the math gets aggressive. Recovered hours don't sit idle. They unlock new capacity. At 20 hours recovered per client × 8 clients = 160 hours reclaimed per year. At $200/hour, that's capacity worth $32,000 - but the real number is higher, because you can either:

**Option A: Take on more clients.** 160 hours at 25 hours per client = 6.4 additional engagements. At a blended $20,000 per engagement, that's $128,000 of potential new revenue.

**Option B: Deepen the existing engagements.** Use the recovered hours on strategic, high-leverage work. Retainer rates go up. Scope expands. Fees climb 25-40% on existing clients.

**Option C: Charge for the asset, not the hour.** Productised offers - diagnostic assessments, frameworks applied to a client's specific situation, strategic reviews - priced by output rather than input. Fee per engagement typically 2-3x the hourly equivalent because the client is buying the judgment, not the time.

Most operators I've watched do this end up running a mix. Call the blended annualised uplift $80,000 in year one, rising.

## The 3-Year Model

Run it forward three years with conservative assumptions. Knowledge base growth compounds - every engagement teaches the base something new, which makes the next engagement more leveraged.

| Year | Recovered hours | Capacity uplift | Asset-priced offers | Total delta |
|------|----------------|-----------------|---------------------|-------------|
| 1 | $32,000 | $80,000 | $0 | **$112,000** |
| 2 | $40,000 | $110,000 | $60,000 | **$210,000** |
| 3 | $48,000 | $140,000 | $140,000 | **$328,000** |

Three-year cumulative delta: **$650,000** on a $6,400 investment. That's a ~100x multiple in a single career over a span every reader will live through.

The numbers scale with your hourly rate. At $400/hour, double everything. At $100/hour, halve everything. The ratio holds. The 100x multiple holds. The only variable is whether you spend the upfront 32 hours.
